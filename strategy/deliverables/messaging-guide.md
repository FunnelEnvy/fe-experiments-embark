# Embark: Messaging Guide

## Positioning Statement

Financial advisory and consulting for mid-market and PE-backed companies that accelerates financial clarity and execution by deploying Big 4 Alumni consultants who stay, perform, and care, backed by an NPS of 93 and 9% attrition.

| "Embark isn't your ordinary consulting firm. Traditional. Old-fashioned. Beige in thought and action." | "Solving complex business challenges with industry-leading expertise." |

**Voice in five words:** Confident, hospitable, unpretentious, outcome-driven, human.

**Voice rules:**
- Always use first person plural ("we," "our," "us"). Third person only for PR/media.
- Never use unqualified superlatives: "industry-leading," "world-class," "best-in-class." Confidence comes from specificity (NPS 93, 9% attrition, 26 offices), not adjectives.
- Lead with the pain, follow with the proof, close with the human element.
- Texas SBPA compliance is non-negotiable: no "accounting" in service names, no "CPA" in titles, no "expert/specialist" (use "advisor/consultant"). See the Language Guidance section for the full list.
- Avoid fear-based selling. Frame problems as solvable, not catastrophic. The emotional register is "we've got this," not "you're in trouble."
- Proof before claim. If a message lacks a supporting data point, it goes in body copy, not in the headline.
