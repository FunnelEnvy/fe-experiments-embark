## Audience

**Primary buyer:** The person experiencing the finance leadership gap. This is NOT the CFO (the CFO is the role being filled). The buyer is typically:

- CEO or COO at a mid-market company that just lost their CFO or Controller
- PE sponsor/operating partner who needs finance leadership injected into a portfolio company
- Board member or audit committee chair concerned about financial controls during a transition
- VP Finance or Controller who suddenly inherited responsibilities beyond their scope

**Buyer's trigger events:**

1. CFO or Controller departure (voluntary or involuntary)
2. PE acquisition requiring upgraded financial reporting and controls
3. Audit season approaching with no senior finance leadership
4. M&A transaction creating integration complexity beyond current team capacity
5. Board or investor pressure on financial visibility and reporting quality

**What they're measured on:** Business continuity, reporting accuracy, audit outcomes, board/sponsor confidence, and ultimately: time-to-resolution (how fast the gap gets filled without compounding damage).

**Emotional state:** Urgency mixed with vulnerability. They know the gap is costing them. Every week without senior finance leadership increases risk: missed closes, reporting gaps, audit findings, eroding board confidence. They need to feel "this is solvable now" not "you're in trouble." The emotional register is competent urgency, not fear.

**Search behavior:** These buyers are solution-aware. They know they need interim finance help. They're comparing providers, not discovering the category. Page should skip category education and go straight to "why Embark" and "how fast."
