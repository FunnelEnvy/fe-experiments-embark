# Battle Card: CrossCountry Consulting

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Threat Level:** Medium

## In One Sentence

D.C.-based advisory firm with 1,000+ employees specializing in technology-enabled transformation, accounting/risk, and transactions, competing on culture with "Best Places to Work" awards.

## Their Positioning

CrossCountry positions as "The future ready business, now within reach" with emphasis on "human-centered transformation." They are the only competitor in this set that directly competes on the culture and employee experience dimension, accumulating Glassdoor Best Places to Work, Consulting Magazine, Inc. 5000, and Globee Awards. Service lines span accounting/risk, technology-enabled transformation, AI strategy, and transactions.

## What's Actually True

The "Best Places to Work" awards are real and third-party verified. The technology partnership portfolio is strong and overlaps with Embark's (NetSuite, Coupa, Workday, Sage Intacct, OneStream, FloQast). The CDS acquisition expanded their capabilities. Inc. 5000 listing confirms growth trajectory. 1,000+ employees is a substantial bench.

## Where They're Weak

- Awards are qualitative. No published NPS, attrition, or retention metrics. "Best Places to Work" is a different claim than "our people stay and our clients are satisfied." The data gap is real.
- D.C. headquarters and government adjacency may signal less depth in energy, industrial, or Texas/Southern markets.
- Heavy technology transformation positioning may not suit buyers needing pure financial advisory, audit readiness, or foundational accounting work.
- Less visible revenue growth trajectory than Embark. No published revenue or growth data.

## Where We Win

- **Quantified proof vs. award logos.** Embark's NPS 93, 9% attrition, and 91% retention are published numbers. CrossCountry has awards but no comparable data. The question for buyers: do you want third-party logos or first-party metrics?
- **Energy, real estate, and Southern markets.** Embark's Dallas HQ and 26 offices (many in energy/Southern markets) provide deeper regional coverage.
- **Pure financial advisory.** When the engagement is financial reporting, audit readiness, or Office of the CFO work, Embark's positioning and case study proof are more directly relevant.
- **Revenue growth trajectory.** Embark's $7M to $149M growth (2018-2023) is published and verifiable. CrossCountry's growth is inferred from Inc. 5000 listing without specifics.

## Where We Lose

- When the buyer's primary need is technology-enabled transformation, AI strategy, or procurement optimization. CrossCountry's positioning and partnerships are purpose-built for those engagements.
- When the buyer values third-party "Best Places to Work" awards over self-reported satisfaction metrics.
- When the engagement involves government-adjacent work or D.C.-based operations.

## Landmines

Their sales team will lead with the award portfolio (Glassdoor, Consulting Magazine, Inc. 5000). Counter: "Awards are third-party logos. We publish the data: NPS 93, 9% attrition, 91% client retention. What's their NPS? What's their consultant attrition rate?"

They may position their technology transformation capabilities as broader than Embark's. Counter: "We share many of the same technology partnerships (NetSuite, FloQast, Workday). The difference is our people: Big 4 Alumni advisors with a 9% attrition rate who stay on your engagement."
