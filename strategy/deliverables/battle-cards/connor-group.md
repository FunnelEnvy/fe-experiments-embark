# Battle Card: Connor Group

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Threat Level:** Medium

## In One Sentence

Silicon Valley advisory firm claiming #1 IPO advisory position in the Bay Area (54% market share), serving high-profile tech clients like OpenAI, HubSpot, and Liquid Death across IPO, M&A, and technology implementation.

## Their Positioning

Connor Group's H1 is "Ready, set, breakthrough!" They position as professional services for ambitious companies, leading with IPO advisory dominance: $4.0 trillion in client valuation helped to IPO, 750+ M&A deals and IPOs, 54% Bay Area IPO market share. The client roster is a credibility engine: OpenAI's VP of Finance testimonial ("set up an ERP within 90 days"), HubSpot's Head of Business Systems, Liquid Death's Head of Technical Accounting. They list AI & Advanced Automation as their top solution.

## What's Actually True

The tech client logos are real and impressive: OpenAI, HubSpot, Liquid Death, Vytalize Health, Nusano, AngioSafe. The IPO market share claim (54% Bay Area) is specific enough to be verifiable. Testimonials include named individuals from marquee companies with concrete outcomes. 2,000+ clients and 700+ employees represent meaningful scale in the tech advisory niche. 66% of their team has worked in industry (not just Big 4), which is a differentiated talent model. 40+ software partnerships, including certified Workday implementation partner.

## Where They're Weak

- Heavy Silicon Valley/tech concentration limits relevance in non-tech verticals. Energy, healthcare, real estate, financial services buyers have limited reason to engage Connor Group.
- IPO market cyclicality creates revenue concentration risk. In IPO downturns, their core business contracts.
- Limited national coverage: handful of offices (Silicon Valley, SF, NYC, Salt Lake City, Boston) vs. Embark's 26.
- Engagement model appears optimized for transactions, not ongoing advisory.
- No published culture metrics (NPS, attrition, retention).

## Where We Win

- **Non-tech industries.** Any engagement in energy, healthcare, financial services, real estate, or other non-tech verticals is Embark territory.
- **National coverage.** 26 offices vs. approximately 5. For companies headquartered outside coastal tech hubs, Embark has geographic presence.
- **Ongoing advisory.** Embark's engagement models include retainer, interim, and fractional leadership for long-term relationships. Connor Group's model appears transaction-oriented.
- **Culture proof.** NPS 93, 9% attrition, 91% retention. Connor Group publishes no comparable metrics.

## Where We Lose

- When the buyer is a tech company heading for IPO and Connor Group's 54% Bay Area market share and OpenAI/HubSpot credentials are decisive. That combination of social proof and track record is difficult to counter in the tech IPO context.
- When the engagement is specifically M&A transaction services in the tech sector.
- When the "cool factor" of their client roster (OpenAI, Liquid Death) matters to the buyer's perception of advisor quality.

## Landmines

Their sales team will lead with the client logos: OpenAI, HubSpot, Liquid Death. Counter: "Impressive clients in tech. What's their track record in [buyer's industry]? We have 17 published case studies across 15+ industries, including named client outcomes with quantified results."

They may cite the 54% Bay Area IPO market share as proof of dominance. Counter: "Market share in Bay Area tech IPOs is a narrow metric. We serve PE-backed and mid-market companies across 19 industries from 26 offices. What matters is whether they have depth outside of tech transactions."

They may highlight their AI-forward positioning. Counter: "We offer Generative AI consulting too. But advisory outcomes are driven by the people doing the work. Our NPS is 93. Our consultant attrition is 9%. What are their numbers?"
