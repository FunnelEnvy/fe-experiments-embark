# Battle Card: Baker Tilly

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Threat Level:** Medium

## In One Sentence

A $6.8B global advisory, tax, and assurance firm with 50,000+ employees across 700+ offices, recently combined with Moss Adams, targeting the middle market with enterprise-scale resources.

## Their Positioning

Baker Tilly positions as a "leading advisory, tax and assurance firm" with "coast-to-coast and global advantage." The core claim: "One firm with the resources, expertise and vision to better serve the middle market." The scale is their differentiator: $6.8B combined firm income, 50,000+ global workforce, 700+ offices across 147 territories, and a Top 10 position in the largest international networks. Recent acquisitions (Berkowitz Pollack Brant, KraftCPAs, Sockeye, Moss Adams) signal aggressive inorganic growth.

## What's Actually True

The scale is real and verifiable: $6.8B revenue, 50K+ people, 700+ offices. The Moss Adams combination is a meaningful expansion of US coverage. Middle-market positioning is genuine, with dedicated advisory practices serving that segment. Global reach through 147 territories provides international coverage that Embark cannot match. The acquisition pace confirms strategic growth investment.

## Where They're Weak

- Scale creates bureaucracy. Mid-market clients may feel lost in a $6.8B, 50K+ person firm. The question is whether the local partner you met in the pitch meeting is the person actually running your engagement.
- Post-acquisition integration creates internal turbulence. Multiple simultaneous acquisitions (4+ in recent years) mean cultural integration is ongoing.
- Advisory competes internally with audit/tax for resources, attention, and senior talent. During audit busy season, advisory engagements may be deprioritized.
- No published culture or satisfaction metrics.

## Where We Win

- **Boutique feel with senior practitioners.** Embark provides the personal engagement experience of a smaller firm: NPS 93, 91% retention, named clients who cite personal service and flexibility. A $6.8B firm cannot replicate that relationship model.
- **Speed and flexibility.** Embark's engagement model is built for mid-market speed: no long contracts, no bloated fees, flexible engagement structures. Baker Tilly's enterprise processes may be slower to mobilize.
- **Culture proof.** NPS 93, 9% attrition. Baker Tilly publishes revenue and scale metrics but no satisfaction or retention data.
- **Advisory focus.** At Embark, advisory is the business, not a secondary revenue line competing with audit and tax for attention.

## Where We Lose

- When the buyer needs global coverage. 700+ offices in 147 territories vs. Embark's 26 US offices. This is not close.
- When the buyer wants audit + tax + advisory from a single firm. Baker Tilly's bundle is comprehensive.
- When the buyer's board or PE sponsor requires a Top 10 brand name for credibility. Baker Tilly's institutional weight is a decision factor at the board level.

## Landmines

Their sales team will lead with scale: "$6.8B firm, 50,000 people, 700+ offices." Counter: "Scale is relevant if you need global coverage. For mid-market advisory in the US, the question is: who's actually on your engagement? Our NPS is 93. Our consultant attrition is 9%. Our clients retain us at 91%. What happens to your engagement when their audit practice gets busy?"

They may position the Moss Adams combination as expanded US coverage. Counter: "Integration takes years. Which firm's culture, processes, and people will you actually experience? We've built a cohesive team from the ground up: NPS 93 across 26 offices."
