# Battle Card: RSM

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Threat Level:** Medium

## In One Sentence

A 17,000+ person middle market professional services firm operating across 150+ countries, offering assurance, tax, and consulting under the tagline "The power of being understood."

## Their Positioning

RSM positions as "Assurance, tax and consulting services for middle market leaders" with "The power of being understood" as their tagline. They explicitly target the middle market and invest in thought leadership through proprietary research: the Middle Market Business Index and Real Economy reports. The transatlantic merger with RSM UK expanded their global footprint. They balance scale (17K+ people, 150+ countries) with middle-market empathy.

## What's Actually True

The middle market focus is genuine and long-standing. The Middle Market Business Index is a recognized research product that provides thought leadership credibility. Global reach through RSM International across 150+ countries is real. 17,000+ professionals is a substantial bench. The transatlantic merger with RSM UK expanded capabilities and coverage. Employee testimonials suggest internal culture investment.

## Where They're Weak

- Full-service CPA model means advisory talent competes internally with audit and tax. During audit season, advisory resources may be pulled.
- 17K+ people creates an institutional feel. "The power of being understood" claims empathy, but the size of the firm may contradict the personal touch.
- "The power of being understood" is subjective and unquantified. No NPS, attrition, or retention data backs the relationship claim.
- Transatlantic merger integration may create internal distractions.
- Consulting may be perceived as a bolt-on to the core audit practice rather than a standalone advisory capability.

## Where We Win

- **Personal service proof.** NPS 93, 9% attrition, 91% retention vs. RSM's unquantified "power of being understood." Embark has data; RSM has a tagline.
- **Advisory depth.** Embark's entire business is advisory with Big 4 Alumni practitioners. RSM's consulting competes with audit/tax for talent and attention.
- **Culture data.** Embark publishes culture metrics. RSM publishes market research. Different kinds of proof, and only one answers the question "what's it like to work with them?"
- **Case study outcomes.** 17 published case studies with quantified results vs. RSM's thought leadership reports (Real Economy, Middle Market Business Index), which prove market knowledge but not engagement execution.

## Where We Lose

- When the buyer needs audit + tax + consulting from a single firm. RSM's full-service model is comprehensive.
- When the buyer needs global coverage across 150+ countries. RSM's international network is far broader than Embark's 26 US offices.
- When the buyer's board requires a Top 10 brand name. RSM's institutional credibility is a board-level decision factor.
- When the buyer values industry-specific research and thought leadership. RSM's Middle Market Business Index is a recognized market intelligence product.

## Landmines

Their sales team will lead with middle market expertise and proprietary research (Middle Market Business Index). Counter: "Research proves they understand the market. Our NPS of 93 proves we deliver in it. What percentage of their consulting team comes from Big 4 advisory backgrounds versus rotating from their audit practice?"

They may position "The power of being understood" as a relationship differentiator. Counter: "We agree that understanding matters. That's why we publish our NPS (93), attrition (9%), and retention (91%). Those numbers are the 'power of being understood' in practice. What are RSM's?"
