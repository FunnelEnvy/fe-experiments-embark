# Battle Card: CFGI

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Threat Level:** High

## In One Sentence

PE-backed advisory firm claiming to be the "largest independent accounting advisory firm," serving Fortune 500 companies and PE-backed organizations across 28+ offices globally, with former SEC Chair Jay Clayton on the board.

## Their Positioning

CFGI positions as "Supporting CFOs In All Critical Functions" with the broadest service catalog among independent advisory firms: 21+ service lines including forensic accounting and cybersecurity. Their credibility rests on institutional signals: dual PE backing from The Carlyle Group and CVC, former SEC Chair Jay Clayton on the board, named enterprise logos (Johnson & Johnson, Boston Scientific, Ocean Spray, Revlon), and FloQast Elite Tier partnership.

## What's Actually True

The enterprise client roster is real and verifiable (named logos on the homepage). Carlyle/CVC backing provides genuine institutional weight. Jay Clayton's board presence gives capital markets credibility that no other competitor in this set can match. The global footprint (28+ offices across Americas, Europe, Asia Pacific) is broader than Embark's US-only presence. The service catalog breadth (21+ lines) is the widest among pure-play advisory firms.

## Where They're Weak

- "Largest independent accounting advisory firm" is unverified and may not hold after recent market consolidation. Ask for the source.
- PE ownership by two mega-funds (Carlyle, CVC) creates potential conflicts when serving other PE firms' portfolio companies. A company backed by Bain Capital may not want advisory from a Carlyle/CVC-owned firm.
- 2023 busy-season layoffs surfaced in professional community discussion (Reddit r/Accounting), signaling delivery model stress. Ask about current headcount stability.
- Enterprise-grade positioning means mid-market companies may get deprioritized. The question is whether a mid-market engagement gets the same attention as a Johnson & Johnson engagement.
- No published culture or satisfaction metrics: no NPS, no attrition rate, no client retention data.

## Where We Win

- **Mid-market focus.** Embark is built for mid-market companies. CFGI's enterprise positioning (J&J, Boston Scientific) means mid-market buyers may not be a priority.
- **Culture proof.** NPS 93, 9% attrition, 91% client retention vs. CFGI's unquantified quality claims and 2023 layoff signals.
- **No PE ownership conflicts.** Embark's Parthenon Capital backing is a single-sponsor minority investment. CFGI's dual Carlyle/CVC ownership creates real conflicts for portfolio companies of other PE firms.
- **Consultant stability.** Embark's 9% attrition vs. CFGI's busy-season layoffs.

## Where We Lose

- When the buyer is a Fortune 500 company that needs institutional heft and 21+ service lines.
- When the buyer is a Carlyle or CVC portfolio company. CFGI has a structural advantage.
- When the engagement requires forensic accounting, cybersecurity, or other specialized services outside Embark's core.
- When complex SEC reporting, IPO, or capital markets work is involved and Jay Clayton's regulatory credibility is decisive.
- When the buyer needs global delivery across Americas, Europe, and Asia Pacific.

## Landmines

Their sales team will lead with the "largest independent" claim and the enterprise client roster. Counter: "How does your dual PE ownership affect independence when advising other PE firms' portfolio companies? And what's your consultant retention rate over the last 3 years?"

They may leverage Jay Clayton's name for capital markets credibility. Counter: "We have a former Deloitte Global CEO on our board and 17 published case studies, including Atlas Technical's 4-week SEC filing and Solo Brands' IPO. Board names are credibility signals; case study outcomes are execution proof."
