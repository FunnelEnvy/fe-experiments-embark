## Voice

### Voice Profile

- **Tone:** Confident, warm, direct. Not stiff or corporate. Not casual or irreverent. Sits in a zone between "professional services firm" and "we're people who like doing this." The warmth is genuine, not performative.
- **Person:** First person plural ("we") consistently across homepage, about, services, and landing pages.
- **Complexity:** Accessible. Financial terminology appears where necessary (ASC 606, ASC 842, GAAP, IFRS, SOX) but always in context of solving a problem, not as jargon for credibility. Service descriptions lead with pain, not with acronyms.

- **Emotion:** Confident bordering on aspirational. "Building the best consulting firm in the world." "Happy is good for business." The emotion is conviction, not urgency or fear. They don't sell fear of failure -- they sell the upside of working with people who care.

### Voice Rules for Downstream Skills

Actionable constraints for all content production:

1. **Always use first person plural ("we," "our," "us").** Never refer to Embark in third person in marketing copy. Third person is acceptable only in PR/media contexts.
2. **Never use "industry-leading," "world-class," "best-in-class," or similar unqualified superlatives.** Confidence comes from specificity (NPS 93, 9% attrition, 26 offices), not adjectives. If you can't back it with a proof point, cut it.
3. **Lead with the pain, follow with the proof, close with the human.** Structure: "You're dealing with [specific problem]. We solve it with [specific capability]. Here's what our clients say: [proof point]."
4. **Texas SBPA compliance is non-negotiable.** Every piece of copy must be checked against the Banned Terms list. No exceptions, no "it sounds better" overrides. When in doubt, use the approved alternatives from L0 Glossary.
5. **Avoid fear-based selling.** Embark's voice is built on conviction and warmth, not urgency or anxiety. Frame problems as solvable, not catastrophic. The emotional register is "we've got this" not "you're in trouble."
6. **Use the client's language, not Embark's jargon, in headlines and ads.** Customer Language entries from this Language Bank should drive headline copy. Company Language entries are for body copy and about pages.
7. **Proof before claim.** If a message in the hierarchy doesn't have a proof point ID, it doesn't go in the headline, the ad, or the email subject. Unproven claims go in body copy only, tagged as vulnerable.
8. **Required disclaimer in appropriate contexts:** "Embark is not a CPA firm." Must appear in footer or equivalent position on any materials that could be interpreted as implying CPA services.
